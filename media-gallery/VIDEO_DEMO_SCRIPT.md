# BLACK SWAN FORGE — 90-Second Demo Video Script

Use this to record the required demo video. Upload the final video to YouTube as **Unlisted** or to Loom, then paste that URL into the hackathon submission form.

## Recommended title

BLACK SWAN FORGE — Finding Hidden City-Collapse Chains Before Disaster Strikes

## Recommended video description

BLACK SWAN FORGE is an adversarial resilience simulator for cities. It models power, water, telecom, hospitals, transport, fuel, payments, emergency response, and civic systems as one dependency graph. The prototype searches for small shocks that create large cascading failures, explains the causal chain, and proves low-cost interventions that prevent collapse.

GitHub: https://github.com/Ananya6Daitkar/Swanforge

## Recording setup

- Length: 90 to 120 seconds.
- Format: landscape, 1080p if possible.
- Start from the app home screen.
- Open the browser at `http://localhost:3000`.
- Keep the mouse movement slow and intentional.
- Use `08-video-thumbnail.png` as the video thumbnail if the platform allows thumbnails.

## Script

### 0:00–0:10 — Opening hook

Say:

> Most disaster systems prepare for failures we already expect. BLACK SWAN FORGE asks the opposite question: what hidden chain could turn a small infrastructure failure into a citywide collapse?

Screen:

- Show the BLACK SWAN FORGE title/home screen.
- Slowly move to the city graph.

### 0:10–0:25 — Explain the system in simple terms

Say:

> A city is not one system. Power, water, telecom, hospitals, payments, transport, fuel, and emergency response all depend on each other. SwanForge turns those dependencies into an executable model.

Screen:

- Show the live simulation graph.
- Point out several connected services.

### 0:25–0:45 — Run the live demo

Say:

> Now I run the 90-second demo. The system simulates how a quiet failure propagates through delayed dependencies, backup limits, and recovery rules.

Screen:

- Click `Run 90-Second Demo`.
- Let the cascade/timeline advance.
- Pause briefly when failures appear.

### 0:45–1:05 — Show the moonshot idea

Say:

> The core idea is adversarial resilience. Instead of asking what failure is most likely, SwanForge searches for the failure that would hurt most if reality were adversarial.

Screen:

- Open the `Adversarial Search` tab.
- Click or show the search running.
- Show the score and generation progress.

### 1:05–1:25 — Show technical proof

Say:

> This is not a static dashboard. The prototype compares evolutionary search against baselines, runs robustness tests across uncertain worlds, and bridges to an IEEE-14 power-grid cascade model.

Screen:

- Open the `Research Evidence` tab.
- Show the 1.90× result, 19 out of 20 wins, robustness result, and 259 MW IEEE-14 cascade.

### 1:25–1:40 — Show intervention value

Say:

> The output is not just a scary scenario. SwanForge explains the causal chain and identifies the cheapest interventions that prevent collapse before disaster happens.

Screen:

- Return to the demo result or intervention section.
- Show the recommended intervention.

### 1:40–1:55 — Closing

Say:

> The long-term vision is a new civic safety layer: cities, hospitals, ports, campuses, and grids continuously stress-tested against black-swan failure paths before the real world discovers them.

Screen:

- End on the title screen or research evidence screen.

## Shorter 60-second version

> BLACK SWAN FORGE is an adversarial resilience simulator for cities. Most disaster systems prepare for the failures we already expect. SwanForge searches for the hidden chains we do not expect: small infrastructure shocks that cascade through power, water, telecom, hospitals, transport, fuel, payments, and emergency response.
>
> The prototype turns a city into an executable dependency graph. It runs cascading-failure simulations, uses evolutionary search to find worst-case scenarios, minimizes the causal chain, and then tests low-cost interventions.
>
> The evidence is computed by the system: evolutionary search finds severe collapse paths about 1.90× better than random search, wins 19 out of 20 benchmark seeds, runs robustness testing across uncertain worlds, and connects the model to an IEEE-14 power-grid cascade with 259 MW of unserved load.
>
> SwanForge is not a dashboard. It is a black-swan discovery engine for civilization-critical infrastructure.
