# BLACK SWAN FORGE — Submission Copy

## One-line description

An adversarial world model that discovers infrastructure disasters nobody thought to test, minimizes them into executable causal counterexamples, and finds the cheapest intervention that prevents collapse.

## Short description

Cities are systems of systems: telecom enables payments, payments enable fuel, fuel enables hospitals, and roads and data enable emergency response. BLACK SWAN FORGE crash-tests those hidden dependencies. Its deterministic simulation propagates delayed failures across eleven domains; evolutionary search discovers severe cascades; delta debugging removes unnecessary conditions; edge-deletion replay falsifies causal claims; and intervention optimization verifies the lowest-cost repair. In a 20-seed study, adversarial search achieved a 1.90x mean collapse-discovery ratio over random sampling, with 95% CI [1.65, 2.15]. Results are synthetic and plausible only under the model’s assumptions.

## Category claim

**Adversarial Infrastructure Science**: discover unknown cascade → minimize cause → falsify links → optimize repair → verify survival.

## The misunderstood problem

Humanity treats infrastructure resilience as an observation problem. The deeper problem is epistemic: we cannot monitor a failure mechanism nobody has imagined.

## First-principles insight

A small failure can be more dangerous than an obvious catastrophe because redundancy hides consequences long enough for the cascade to cross organizational boundaries.

## Why now

Cities are becoming more software-defined and more interdependent. Simulation, search, causal replay, and public infrastructure benchmarks can now be combined into a practical hypothesis-discovery loop without requiring a paid AI service.

## Demonstration sequence

1. Establish the healthy synthetic city.
2. Fail telecom authentication.
3. Reveal delayed payment and fuel propagation.
4. Show hospital capacity crossing its safety threshold.
5. Display the event-backed minimal causal chain.
6. Delete a causal link and replay.
7. Optimize the repair.
8. Add offline emergency fuel authorization.
9. Replay the counterfactual.
10. Show the hospital surviving.

## Measured evidence

- 28 city components and 46 delayed dependencies across eleven domains.
- 61 collapse cases from 120 evolutionary simulations versus 43 from random sampling.
- Across 20 seeds: 1.90x mean evolutionary/random discovery ratio; 95% CI [1.65, 2.15]; evolutionary wins 19/20 seeds.
- Cascade persists in 68.75% of 64 parameter perturbations and is honestly labeled SENSITIVE.
- IEEE-14 DC bridge produces a derived-limit N-1 cascade that islands approximately 259 MW.
- 22 unit/property tests, three browser-level flows, and a passing production build.

## Scientific boundary

This is a synthetic research prototype. It does not predict real cities. Dependencies and parameters are demonstrative. Real deployment requires validated infrastructure data and domain experts. Every generated result is **plausible under the assumptions of this model**.
