# Research Story

## Research question

Can an executable cross-infrastructure model automatically discover a small, delayed cascade that domain-by-domain planning misses, then identify a minimal falsifiable repair?

## First-principles insight

Catastrophic outcomes need not begin with catastrophic shocks. Redundancy can hide early loss while a dependency crosses administrative domains. Therefore the useful object is not a risk score but a minimal executable counterexample: a reproducible initial condition, event-by-event causal trace, measurable collapse, and counterfactual that breaks it.

## Novelty claim

The prototype unifies four operations in one evidence-preserving loop: executable cross-domain propagation, novelty-aware adversarial scenario search, replay-based causal minimization, and minimum-cost intervention validation. The claim is methodological, not that the synthetic scenarios predict real events.

## Evaluation metrics

- Outcome: severity, critical-service availability, hospital/water/emergency capacity, recovery time.
- Structure: failed nodes, causal depth, domains crossed, delayed impact.
- Search: best/average fitness, unique cascade signatures, initial shock complexity.
- Explanation: trace events must exist in the simulation ledger and connect through causal-parent IDs.
- Repair: cost and before/after safety-predicate satisfaction.

## Baselines

Useful baselines are uniform random shock sampling; single-domain failure injection; breadth-first topology traversal without dynamic state; and greedy intervention ranking without replay. Evaluation should compare severe unique cascades per simulation budget and minimal counterexample size.

## Difference from adjacent practice

Chaos engineering injects failures into a deployed or staged system to test known resilience hypotheses. Black Swan Forge searches a synthetic hypothesis space before deployment and minimizes discovered counterexamples. Ordinary digital twins primarily estimate or mirror system state; this model is deliberately adversarial and asks which small assumptions can produce a critical outcome.

## Potential paper direction

A paper could formalize constrained novelty search over hybrid infrastructure models, compare delta-debugging explanation faithfulness against attribution methods, and test whether domain experts judge minimized traces as more actionable than unminimized Monte Carlo scenarios. A validated study would require real topology under secure governance and expert-designed physical constraints.
